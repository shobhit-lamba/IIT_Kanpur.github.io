Indian Institute of Technology, Kanpur

About the Institute...

Indian Institute of Technology Kanpur is a public technical and research university located in Kanpur, Uttar Pradesh, India. It was declared to be an Institute of National Importance by the Government of India under the Institutes of Technology Act.

Established in 1959 as one of the first Indian Institutes of Technology, the institute was created with the assistance of a consortium of nine US research universities as part of the Kanpur Indo-American Programme (KIAP).

Where it stands?

IIT Kanpur is one of the most sought after colleges in India. It belongs to the league of prestigious IITs like IIT Bombay, IIT Delhi, IIT Madras, and IIT Kharagpur. IIT Kanpur is the first generation of IITs established in India. This fact is also reflected in its rankings. It has consistently been among the top 5 in NIRF’s rankings.

Campus

We are mini-academic –city, a self sustained, lush-green campus spread across 1055 acres with about 14,000 inhabitants including faculty members, students, staff and helping hands. The campus is well-equipped with infrastructure which caters to our academic, culinary, residential, recreational, shopping and other needs. For visitors, we have finished Apartments and a Guests House.

We experience all seasons-the scorching heat of the summer, the wet, the humid afternoons of the rainy season, and the chilly and hazy nights of the winter when sneaks from the street lamps, filtered through the mist, offer breathtaking, visual treat. Our hallmark is the presence of numerous peacocks on Campus, often lurching on the green grounds or resting on high branches. One does not mind the hardwork amidst the consistently emerging and calming, innate picturesque ambience of the campus, and while witnessing the twilight, one eagerly awaits the promise the next day holds.

   Antaragni

Antaragni, the cultural festival of IIT Kanpur, marks an eruption of cultural flair and creative exuberance that is the hallmark of our national diversity in general, and our institute in particular, thus defining the purpose it seeks to fulfill. Over the years, Antaragni has expanded and evolved into the biggest cultural extravaganza organised by students of an institute. The fest has experienced a breath-taking 130,000-strong footfall with more than 25,000,000 eyeballs.

   Techkriti

Techkriti is Asia's largest technical and entrepreneurial fest organized by the students of IIT Kanpur. The word Techkriti is derived from tech (technology) and the Sanskrit kriti (creation). Students and academia at the festival can attend talks by professionals in various fields from India and abroad. Speakers at Techkriti have included aerospace scientist and former Indian president A. P. J. Abdul Kalam, Free Software Foundation founder Richard Stallman and Rakesh Sharma, the only Indian to date to travel in space.

 Udghosh

IIT Kanpur is equipped with the state of of the art facility in the sector of sports. Udghosh organises 22 sports events with a huge participation of both boys and girls from over 200 colleges across the country. Over 2500 students participants take part in a broad spectrum of fine sports events and informal events, followed by various other attractions like pro-Nights, comedy nights etc.
